How Online Debates
Work

We, at MUNLine, host debates completely online. Although there are some slight differences from an online debate to a physical conference, the major components of MUN are included in our debates. 

For our debates we will be using an online meeting software called Big Blue Button. This online platform allows delegates to truly experience the features of a physical conference without having to travel anywhere. The key features of Big Blue Button include a chat box, a presentation place, a polling function, and various emoticons. Overall, Big Blue Button will allow delegates to express their ideas and come up with solutions to the world’s most pressing problems.

Below is a guide on how to use the key features of Big Blue Button.


No Installation Required


There is no need to download any sort of software in order to participate in our debates as Big Blue Button runs within your own web browser. However, we do recommend downloading the latest version of Google Chrome or FireFox just because they are the most compatible browsers to use with Big Blue Button.


How to enter into a Debate Room


A staff member of MUNLine will email you with your country assignment as well as the debate link. However, we will remind you that the debate link will not work until around 15 minutes before the debate actually starts. Once the time comes, you can enter the debate room by simply clicking the link. Once you’ve clicked the link you should be directed to a page with this option of entering your name.


PLEASE DO NOT ENTER YOUR ACTUAL NAME. Instead enter the name of the country that you’re representing. Once you’ve typed out the name of your country you can hit join and this page should appear.


Big Blue Button will prompt you to select between the two options of Microphone or Listen only. Microphone allows you to unlock your mic and speak while listen only locks your mic and doesn’t allow for you to speak. Delegates, PLEASE CLICK MICROPHONE. This will allow you to participate in the debate with speeches, POIs, questions, motions, and so forth. After selecting the microphone option you will be prompted to take a private echo test. Please note that this is used to make sure that your audio is working.


On the right hand side of your screen you can see the attendees list and you can decide to start a private chat with any of your fellow delegates simply by clicking on their name. Right next to the attendees list is the public chat which is normally used for any questions that you may have for the chair or moderators. Also, if you’d like to ask a POI to a fellow delegate, but your microphone isn't working then you can simply type your POI into the public chat. To the right of the chat is the presentation screen where any slideshows, presentations, or resolutions will be uploaded for your viewing. Underneath the presentation screen are three buttons: microphone, leave call, and video. During debate we ask delegates to always have their video off as the discussions are always just audio-based. If you would like to speak then you can just click the mic option, but please be mindful to keep yourself muted while the chair or other delegates speak in order to be respectful.
