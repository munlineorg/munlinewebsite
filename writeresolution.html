How To Write A
Resolution

A resolution is, according to the United Nations, a “formal expression of the opinion or will of United Nations organs.” Once it is approved, it outlines formal UN recommendations on a particular topic. A resolution has 3 sections: header, preambulatory clauses and operative clauses.

Header

A header should include: 
Name of the Committee 
Topic under discussion 
List of sponsors: The sponsors of a resolution are the main countries that authored the resolution and want to see it passed. 
List of signatories: The signatories of a resolution want to see it discussed, agree with some ideas of the resolution, but may not agree with everything expressed in it.

Preambulatory clauses

The Pre-ambulatory clauses provide background on the topic and clarify why we need to discuss the problem today. 

They can include the following information:
● References to the UN charter 
● Past UN resolutions or conventions
● Information on the problem 
● Statements by UN officials: 
● Actions taken by non-governmental organizations excluding the UN 
● Actions taken by UN member states 

Preambulatory clauses start with a preambulatory phrase, which is underlined. You should end each clause with a comma. Some examples of Preambulatory phrases, taken from Carthage College, are:

 Expressing its appreciation Noting with satisfaction
Alarmed by Expressing its satisfaction Noting with approval

Operative Clauses

Operative clauses are the proposed solutions to the problem and as such, are action oriented. Clauses start with verbs/actions which are called Operative Phrases and can vary in order of severity (e.g. a list from most severe to least severe could be: Considers, Requests, Calls Upon, Urges, Decides, Demands). Clauses end with a semicolon. A list of some Operative Phrases (which should be underlined in the resolution), taken from Carthage College, are:
Accepts
 Draws the attention Proclaims
Affirms Emphasizes Reaffirms
Approves Further invites Recommends
Authorizes Encourages Regrets
Calls Expresses its appreciation Reminds
Calls upon Expresses its hope Requests
Condemns Further invites Solemnly affirms
Confirms Further proclaims Strongly condemns
Congratulates Further reminds Supports
Considers Further recommends Takes note of
Declares accordingly
 Further requests Transmits
Deplores Further resolves Trusts
Designates Note

When choosing an operative phrase, it is crucial to consider the powers of each committee (only the security council’s resolutions can be binding) and how other delegates will react.

Sample Resolution

(Note: the resolution included below is for sampling purposes and as such, is shorter than what a MUN resolution should look like and thus its solutions are more simplistic)


General Assembly First Committee 
Topic: “Minimizing the problems posed by small arms in conflicts” 
Sponsors: United States, France, Sweden Signatories: Guatemala, United Kingdom, Spain, Ecuador, Greece, Uruguay, India, Finland. 

To the General Assembly, 
Deeply disturbed that domestic weapons production and arms proliferation in developing countries is growing at a fast pace, 
Alarmed by the fact that small arms and light weapons are readily available, and as such they can be used by militias and gangs to further conflicts and increase the loss of life, 
Taking into account actions taken by The United Nations Office of Disarmament Affairs (UNODA), which supports Member States in order to develop methods of disarmament including dialogue, transparency and confidence-building measures, 
Fully aware that small arms make enlisting children as soldiers easier, and that Secretary-general has expressed that this“cause[s] lasting damage to the children themselves, and to their communities and societies to which they belong”, 

1. Condemns all enlistment of child soldiers in armies; 

a. However this does not mean any direct harm to the society, government, or economy of those states which suffer from child soldiers;

2. Encourages states to destroy obsolete and potentially destabilizing weapons by;

a. Promoting any other resolutions committed more specifically to this goal;

b. Continuing to provide support in disarmament measures through the UNODA;

3. Requests all countries to thoughtfully consider the parties that they are supplying small arms to and weather this supply is exacerbating conflict;
